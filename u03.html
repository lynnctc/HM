Management & Motivation

管理與激勵

You cannot force anyone to care — motivation comes from a motive inside the person. What a manager controls is the environment: rewards, fairness, goals, and meaning. In this unit we stack up the classic motivation theories, bust the myths, and learn to motivate five very different generations working the same ward.

你無法強迫任何人在乎——動機來自人心裡的「動因」。管理者能掌握的是環境：報酬、公平、目標與意義。本單元把經典動機理論疊起來看、破解迷思，並學會激勵同一個病房裡五個截然不同的世代。

§1

Motives and Rewards

動因與報酬

A motive is “a need or desire that causes a person to act”; motivation is the process of providing one. Managers exert influence — changing job descriptions, schedules, working conditions, teams — but they cannot force a person to act. And not everyone's “drive” points at the job: some people's real motivation lives in family, school or hobbies. Recognizing that helps you read behavior that looks counterproductive.

「動因」是促使人行動的需要或欲望；「激勵」就是提供動因的過程。管理者能施加影響——調整職務內容、班表、工作條件、團隊——但無法「強迫」人行動。而且不是每個人的驅力都指向工作：有些人的動機在家庭、進修或嗜好。理解這點，才能讀懂那些看似消極的行為。

Extrinsic rewards 外在報酬

  • Money: pay, bonuses 金錢：薪資、獎金
  • Benefits: health, time off, retirement 福利：保險、休假、退休金
  • Flexible schedules, promotions, status, supportive culture 彈性班表、升遷、地位、支持性文化

Intrinsic rewards 內在報酬（Manion 五項）

  • Healthy relationships 健康的人際連結
  • Meaningful work — feeling you make a difference 有意義的工作
  • Competence — performing at or above standard 勝任感
  • Choice — voice and participation 選擇與參與
  • Progress — milestones celebrated 進展被看見
§2

Needs-Based Theories

需求理論

Click each level of Maslow's hierarchy to see what it means for a hospital workforce — then compare the three sibling theories in the tabs.

點 Maslow 金字塔的每一層，看它在醫院職場的意義——再用頁籤比較三個親戚理論。

ERG theory compresses Maslow into three bands — Existence, Relatedness, Growth — and drops the strict ladder: several needs can operate at once, and when growth is frustrated, people regress and demand more relatedness or existence rewards instead.

ERG 理論把 Maslow 壓成三層——生存（E）、關係（R）、成長（G）——並取消嚴格的階梯：多個需求可同時作用；成長受挫時人會「退而求其次」，轉而要求更多關係或生存報酬。

Two-factor theory: hygiene factors (pay, policy, supervision, conditions) don't motivate — but their absence demotivates. True motivators are achievement, recognition, the work itself, responsibility, growth. Implication: fixing the parking lot stops complaints; it will not create passion.

雙因子理論：保健因子（薪資、制度、督導、環境）不會產生激勵——但缺了會消滅士氣。真正的激勵因子是成就、肯定、工作本身、責任、成長。啟示：把停車場修好能止住抱怨，但不會點燃熱情。

Acquired needs theory: through life experience people develop a dominant need — for achievement (stretch goals, feedback), affiliation (harmony, belonging), or power (influence, either personal or institutional). Match assignments accordingly: give your achievement-driven analyst the hard project, not another committee.

後天需求理論：人在成長中發展出主導需求——成就（挑戰性目標與回饋）、親和（和諧與歸屬）、權力（影響力，分個人型與制度型）。指派工作要對頻：讓成就導向的分析師接硬專案，而不是再開一個委員會。

Theory Matcher

理論配對遊戲

Process and management theories explain how motivation works, not just what people need. Read each ward scenario and name the theory at work.

過程理論與管理理論解釋動機「如何」運作，而不只是人需要什麼。讀每個病房情境，指出背後的理論。

Theory 理論Core idea 核心概念
Reinforcement 增強理論Consequences shape behavior: reward the desired, address the undesired 用後果塑造行為
Adams' Equity 公平理論People compare their input/output ratio with others' 與他人比較投入產出比
Vroom's Expectancy 期望理論Motivation = expectancy that effort → performance → valued outcome 努力—績效—報酬的期望鏈
Locke's Goal-Setting 目標設定Specific, challenging goals drive action 明確且有挑戰的目標驅動行為
Theory X / Y（McGregor）X: workers shirk, control them; Y: workers seek growth, enable them X 假設人惰、Y 假設人求成長
Theory Z（Ouchi）Long-term employment + participation → commitment 長期僱用與參與帶來承諾
§4

Engagement — and the Myths

敬業度與動機迷思

Engagement has three faces (Towers Watson): rational — do I understand my role? emotional — do I bring passion? motivational — will I invest discretionary effort? Research links higher engagement to better quality, patient safety, satisfaction and financial performance. Measurement is moving toward people analytics — using people-related data to solve retention, turnover, absenteeism and safety problems with evidence rather than gut feeling.

敬業度有三個面向（Towers Watson）：理性——我清楚我的角色嗎；情感——我帶著熱情嗎；動機——我願意投入額外心力嗎。研究顯示敬業度與品質、病安、滿意度、財務表現正相關。測量正走向人力分析——用人的數據、以證據而非直覺解決留任、離職、缺勤與病安問題。

Myth busters 迷思破解

Money matters — but as Herzberg showed, it's mostly hygiene. A combination of factors motivates; tailored recognition often outperforms cash alone.

錢重要——但如 Herzberg 所示，多屬保健因子。動機來自多因素組合；量身訂做的肯定常勝過單純加錢。

This is the extrinsic incentive bias — we flatter ourselves as intrinsically driven and assume others chase money. Most people are moved by both.

這是「外在誘因偏誤」——我們自認靠內在動機、卻假設別人愛錢。其實多數人內外兼收。

Everyone is motivated by something — it may just not be the job. The manager's challenge is to redirect that energy toward job-related behaviors.

每個人都被「某件事」驅動——只是未必是工作。管理者的挑戰是把那股能量導回工作。

Anyone can become an effective motivator with theory, principle, and the desire to practice.

懂理論、抓原則、願意練習，任何人都能學會激勵他人。

Tailoring rewards signals that you see the individual. The strategies list agrees: expect the best, communicate the big picture, reward desired behavior, keep it FUN (focused, unpredictable, novel), celebrate progress, and give three compliments for every criticism.

量身訂做的獎勵代表你「看見了這個人」。激勵策略也呼應：期待最好、溝通大局、獎勵期望行為、保持 FUN（聚焦、驚喜、新鮮）、慶祝進展、三句稱讚配一句批評。

Five Generations, One Ward

五個世代、同一個病房

Click each generation to expand its workplace character and what actually motivates it. Then note the universal rules that work for everyone.

點各世代展開其職場特質與有效激勵方式，最後記住對所有世代都通用的原則。

before 1945 Traditionalists 傳統世代
Respect authority, duty and sacrifice, strong work ethic, loyal to employer. Motivate with: status, recognition of hard work, hierarchy and tenure-based rewards.敬重權威、重責任與犧牲、忠於雇主。激勵：地位、對苦幹的肯定、依年資的獎酬。
1946–1964 Baby Boomers 嬰兒潮
Goal-driven, measure work ethic in hours, career = identity, competitive but believe in teamwork. Motivate with: being treated as equals, assurance they make a difference, work-life balance.目標驅動、以工時衡量敬業、事業即身分。激勵：被平等對待、確知自己有貢獻、工作生活平衡。
1965–1978 Generation X
Self-reliant, questioning, loyal to a person not an organization, value control of their time. Motivate with: genuine informal managers, growth opportunities, deadlines with freedom on how to reach them.自立、好質疑、忠於「人」而非組織、重視時間自主。激勵：真誠不拘形式的主管、成長機會、給死線但給自由。
1979–1995 Generation Y · Millennials 千禧世代
Team-oriented, need constant feedback, idealistic, structured. Motivate with: coaching and support, collaborative positive interactions, personal fulfillment in the job.團隊導向、需要持續回饋、理想主義。激勵：教練式支持、正向協作、工作中的個人成就感。
after 1995 Generation Z
Tech-savvy, results-focused, strong online collaboration, entrepreneurial, but struggle with face-to-face communication. Motivate with: community impact, consistent feedback, rewards tied to specific goals, workspaces that support their needs.數位原生、結果導向、線上協作強、面對面溝通較弱。激勵：能改善世界的使命感、規律一致的回饋、與明確目標連動的獎酬。
Universal rules
通用原則 Evaluate before implementing; get feedback and act on it; focus on results rather than process; encourage mentorship; recognize when people need extra support; give praise, say thanks, and celebrate when a team gets it right. 先評估再導入；蒐集回饋並真的採取行動；看結果而非流程；推動師徒制；察覺誰需要額外支持；讚美、道謝、團隊做對時好好慶祝。

Taiwan Spotlight 台灣視角

Taiwan's nursing shortage makes this unit practical, not academic: hospital wards routinely mix Boomer supervisors with Gen-Z new graduates, and turnover among first-year nurses is a chronic pain point. Which mix of Manion's intrinsic rewards and generation-appropriate feedback would you design to keep a 24-year-old nurse through her first year?

台灣的護理人力荒讓本單元內容非常實務：病房裡常是嬰兒潮世代的督導帶 Z 世代新人，而新進護理師第一年離職率一直是痛點。你會用哪些 Manion 內在報酬＋世代合拍的回饋設計，讓一位 24 歲護理師撐過第一年？
