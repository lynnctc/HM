Leadership

領導

Are leaders and managers the same people? Health care needs both — the leader looks outward and sets the vision; the manager looks inward and delivers the operation. In this unit we separate the two roles, tour seven contemporary leadership models, match styles to situations, and find out which PDP animal you are.

領導者和管理者是同一種人嗎？醫療機構兩者都需要——領導者向外看、定願景；管理者向內看、顧營運。本單元分清兩種角色、巡覽七種當代領導模型、學會情境配風格，並測出你是哪種 PDP 動物。

§1

Leader vs. Manager

領導者 × 管理者

Leaders spend most of their time communicating and aligning with outside groups — partners, community, vendors, government, media — and setting a vision that motivates employees and stakeholders. Managers focus inside: staffing, patient-centered practices, controlling resources, supervising services, regulatory compliance, developing employees. There is crossover — but the centers of gravity differ.

領導者把多數時間花在與外部群體溝通結盟——夥伴、社區、供應商、政府、媒體——並設定能激勵員工與利害關係人的願景。管理者聚焦內部：人力配置、以病人為中心的作業、資源控制、督導服務、法規遵循、培育員工。兩者有交集，但重心不同。

Three types of leaders: Strategic — defines purpose and vision, aligns people, processes and values (internal + external focus). Network — connects people across disciplines, departments and regions (more external). Operational — has functional oversight responsibilities (more internal).

三類領導者：策略型——定義宗旨與願景、對齊人員／流程／價值（內外兼顧）；網絡型——連結跨領域、跨部門、跨區域的人（偏外部）；營運型——負責功能面督導（偏內部）。

Game: whose job is it? 遊戲：這是誰的工作？

Classify each activity as primarily leadership or management work (Figure 2-1's competency lists).

依 Figure 2-1 的職能清單，判斷每項活動主要屬於「領導」還是「管理」。

§2

Seven Contemporary Leadership Models

七種當代領導模型

No single model wins everywhere; think of them as lenses you can put on. Expand each.

沒有一種模型萬用；把它們當成可替換的鏡片。逐一展開。

Five skills of the well-adjusted leader: self-awareness (know your emotions, strengths, drives), self-regulation (reflect; say no to impulse), motivation (driven to achieve, enjoys challenge), empathy (consider others' feelings), social skills (move people effectively).

五項技能：自我覺察（深知自己的情緒、強弱項與驅力）、自我調節（能反思、能對衝動說不）、動機（追求成就、樂於挑戰）、同理（顧及他人感受）、社交技巧（有效帶動他人）。

People naturally follow someone guided by an internal compass of true purpose. Attributes: confidence, hope, optimism, resilience, integrity, positive values.

人們自然願意追隨依內在羅盤行事的人。特質：自信、希望、樂觀、韌性、高度誠信、正向價值。

The leader who designs acceptance of diversity into the culture succeeds; health care leaders must model cultural competency and mentor people of different backgrounds.

能把「接納多元」設計進組織文化的領導者最成功；醫療領導者要示範文化能力，並提攜不同背景的人。

People work in health care because they want to help. This model flattens the hierarchy and builds a community where everyone contributes to the greater whole.

投身醫療的人多半想助人。此模型打破典型科層，在組織內建立人人貢獻於整體的社群。

Not about religion — emphasizes ethics, values, relationship skills, and work-self balance.

與宗教無關——強調倫理、價值、關係技巧與工作—自我的平衡。

Bounce back from pressure and keep the organization moving; resilience-building practices grow inner strength and perseverance.

承壓後回彈、讓組織持續前進；透過韌性練習累積內在強度與毅力。

The best leaders keep learning and convert new knowledge into further development — essential in a changing health care world.

最好的領導者持續學習，把新知轉化為自我發展——在變動的醫療環境中不可或缺。

Two additions from the updated slide deck. Transformational leadership (Bass's four I's — idealized influence, inspirational motivation, intellectual stimulation, individualized consideration) contrasts with transactional reward-and-exception management; healthcare evidence links the transformational style with better safety climate and lower burnout. Psychological safety (Edmondson) — the shared belief that a team is safe for asking, admitting and dissenting — was discovered in a hospital medication-error study: better teams reported more errors because they dared to. It is the leadership engine behind U6's culture of safety.

更新版投影片新增兩個概念。轉換型領導（Bass 四個 I：理想化影響、激勵鼓舞、智識啟發、個別關懷）與交易型的依約獎懲形成對照；醫療實證顯示轉換型與較佳安全氣候、較低倦怠相關。心理安全感（Edmondson）——團隊成員相信可以安心提問、認錯、唱反調——正是在醫院給藥錯誤研究中被發現的：好團隊「通報更多」錯誤，因為敢報。它是 U6 安全文化背後的領導引擎。

Four Styles — Matched to People

四種風格，配對不同的人

Style 風格Definition 定義Best applied to 適用對象
Coercive 強制型Demanding, power-based 施壓、以權力為基礎Problematic employees 問題員工
Participative 參與型Solicits input, shares decision making 徵詢意見、共同決策Most followers 多數部屬
Pacesetting 標竿型Sets high performance standards 樹立高績效標準Highly competent staff 高勝任度者
Coaching 教練型Focuses on personal development 著重個人發展Top-level talent 頂尖人才
Barriers & challenges
障礙與挑戰 Health care is dynamic: laws and regulations, new technology, and under-representation of women in top positions act as barriers; physicians, the culture of safety, and value-based purchasing are challenges leaders must engage. Ethics needs two lenses at once — biomedical (how the leader's decisions touch patients) and managerial (doing business for the right reasons). 醫療產業高度動態：法規、新科技、女性高階職位比例偏低是「障礙」；醫師關係、安全文化、論值採購是必須面對的「挑戰」。倫理要同時戴兩副眼鏡——生醫倫理（決策如何影響病人）與管理倫理（用正當理由做正確的事）。
§4

Which PDP Animal Are You?

你是哪種 PDP 動物？

The Professional DynaMetric Programs (PDP) framework nicknames five behavioral profiles after animals. Answer five quick questions to see your tendency — then think about which leadership style would motivate you best. (For fun and reflection, not diagnosis.)

PDP 以五種動物比喻行為特質。回答五題看你的傾向——再想想哪種領導風格最能激勵「你」。（供反思用，非正式測評。）

🐯 Tiger 老虎

  • Authority-driven, results- and goal-oriented 權威導向、重實質報酬、目標導向

🦚 Peacock 孔雀

  • Empathic, expressive, self-promoting 同理心強、擅長表達、樂於自我宣傳

🐨 Koala 無尾熊

  • Peace-loving, persistent, patient 愛好和平、持之以恆、忍耐度佳

🦉 Owl 貓頭鷹

  • Precise, professional, rule-following 喜歡精確、重視專業、循規蹈矩

🦎 Chameleon 變色龍

  • Coordinating, adaptable, the team's lubricant 協調性佳、配合度高、團隊潤滑劑
§5

Competency Domains, Protocols & Governance

職能領域、領導守則與治理趨勢

Functional & technical 功能技術

  • Business acumen, strategic vision, decision quality, ethics, problem solving, change management, systems thinking, governance商業敏銳度、策略願景、決策品質、倫理、問題解決、變革管理、系統思考、治理

Interpersonal 人際

  • Communication, motivating, empowerment, group process, conflict resolution, negotiation, presentations溝通、激勵、賦權、群體歷程、衝突解決、談判、簡報

Self-development 自我發展

  • Self-awareness, integrity, lifelong learning, empathy, flexibility, perseverance, work/life balance自我覺察、誠信、終身學習、同理、彈性、毅力、工作生活平衡

Organizational 組織

  • Org design, team building, priority setting, political savvy, measuring performance, developing others, managing culture/diversity組織設計、團隊建立、優先排序、政治敏感度、績效衡量、培育他人、文化與多元管理

Leadership protocols — how a leader should act: professionalism; reciprocal trust and respect; confidence, optimism and passion; being visible; open communication; taking risks; admitting fault; balancing the roles of motivator, vision-setter, analyzer and task-master.

領導守則——領導者該有的樣子：專業、互信互敬、自信樂觀有熱情、常出現在現場、開放溝通、敢冒險、肯認錯，並在激勵者／願景設定者／分析者／督軍四種角色間取得平衡。

Governance trends: boards are getting smaller (avg. ~13), with more outside members, mandatory conflict-of-interest disclosure, strategic (not voluminous) meeting information, serious self-evaluation, focus on system functioning over individual competence — and a goal that shifted from “keep stable” to “manage change.”

治理趨勢：董事會變「小」（平均約 13 人）、外部成員更多、利益衝突必須揭露、會議資料重策略而非厚報表、認真自我評鑑、從看個人能力轉為看系統運作——目標從「維穩」變成「駕馭變革」。

Taiwan Spotlight 台灣視角

Taiwan's Medical Care Act requires hospitals above a certain scale to establish boards (董事會) for foundation-run hospitals; medical-center directors (院長) must be physicians. Compare that with the U.S. trend of professional non-clinician CEOs — a nice discussion: should the top job require a medical license?

台灣《醫療法》要求財團法人醫院設董事會，且醫院院長須具醫師資格。對照美國專業經理人（非臨床背景）擔任 CEO 的趨勢，值得討論：醫院最高職位應不應該要求醫師執照？
