Week 4 · Module 1 管理基礎

Organizational Behavior & Management Thinking

組織行為與管理思維

Before a manager acts, a manager thinks — and most thinking runs on autopilot, complete with shortcuts and blind spots. This week we open the “inner game” of organizational behavior: how schemas, fast-vs-slow thinking, and cognitive biases quietly steer hiring, feedback, and even patient care. Naming the bias is the first step to catching it.

管理者行動之前先思考——而多數思考是自動駕駛，自帶捷徑與盲點。本週打開組織行為的「內在賽局」：基模、快慢思考與認知偏誤如何悄悄左右招募、回饋、甚至病人照護。能叫出偏誤的名字，才抓得住它。

§1

What Is Organizational Behavior?

什麼是組織行為？

Organizational behavior (OB) studies how people act in organizations — “the understanding, prediction, and control of human behavior in organizations” (Luthans). It is interdisciplinary: psychology, social psychology, sociology, anthropology, political science, and lately neuroscience. The prized “soft skills” are OB skills: thinking skills (critical thinking, problem-solving, flexibility) and socio-emotional skills (teamwork, communication, empathy, self-discipline).

組織行為（OB）研究人在組織中的行為——「對組織中人類行為的理解、預測與控制」（Luthans）。它是跨領域學問：心理學、社會心理學、社會學、人類學、政治學，近年還有神經科學。職場搶手的「軟實力」正是 OB 技能：思考技能（批判思考、問題解決、彈性）與社會情緒技能（團隊合作、溝通、同理、自律）。

Individual level 個人層次

  • How perception and personality shape motivation, satisfaction, commitment, learning知覺與人格如何影響動機、滿意、承諾與學習

Group level 群體層次

  • Leadership, teamwork, decision making, power, conflict領導、團隊、決策、權力、衝突

Organization level 組織層次

  • Structure, authority, control systems, HR design, culture, learning, adaptation結構、職權、控制系統、人資設計、文化、學習與調適
Why health care is hard mode
為何醫療是困難模式 Industry pressures (rising costs, competition, consumer demands, chronic illness, patient-safety concerns, labor shortages) meet organizational realities (a professional workforce with high autonomy, exacting work, demands for high reliability). OB is the toolkit for exactly this terrain. 產業壓力（成本上升、競爭、消費者要求、慢性病、病安、缺工）疊加組織現實（高自主的專業人力、精細工作、高可靠度要求）。OB 正是應對這種地形的工具箱。

Mental Representations: the Infrastructure of Thinking

心智表徵：思考的基礎建設

We never deal with the world directly — only with our mental map of it, which is always an approximation. Three structures organize that map:

我們從不直接面對世界——只面對心中的地圖，而地圖永遠是近似值。三種結構組織著這張地圖：

Schemas 基模

  • Knowledge + expectations about a concept 對概念的知識與期待
  • Person 人物 / role 角色 / event 事件 “scripts” 腳本
  • Organizational schemas & framing shape collective understanding 組織基模與框架塑造集體理解

Mental models 心智模型

  • Deep internal images of how the world works 世界如何運作的深層圖像
  • Managers improve organizations by surfacing and challenging them 管理者靠揭露並挑戰心智模型來改善組織

Mind-sets 思維模式

  • Assumptions about one's own abilities 對自身能力的假設
  • Growth: abilities can be developed 成長型：能力可培養
  • Fixed: abilities are set; failure is feared 定型：能力固定、怕失敗
§3

Two Speeds of Thinking

思考的兩種速度

Fast, unconscious, effortless, intuitive. Includes innate and well-learned skills — an experienced triage nurse “just knows” a patient looks wrong. The catch: it jumps to conclusions and believes whatever is easy to recall.

快、無意識、不費力、靠直覺。包含天生與熟練的技能——資深檢傷護理師「一眼就知道」病人不對勁。代價：它愛跳結論，且相信容易想起來的事。

Slow, conscious, controlled, effortful, reasoning. The system that double-checks the drug dose. The catch: it is “lazy” and often fails to challenge the automatic system. Organizations increase deliberate thinking with checklists, second reads, and time-outs.

慢、有意識、受控、費力、講推理。負責覆核藥物劑量的那個系統。代價：它很「懶」，常放任自動系統過關。組織用查檢表、二次判讀、手術暫停（time-out）來強迫深思系統上工。

The processing pipeline: attention (what we notice is filtered by expectations) → perceptual selection → cognitive evaluation (categorize new information) → cognitive consistency (fit it to what we already believe) → behavior. Physician perception, for example, measurably shapes patient–physician interactions and outcomes.

訊息處理流水線：注意（注意什麼由期待過濾）→ 知覺選擇 → 認知評估（把新資訊分類）→ 認知一致性（塞進既有信念）→ 行為。例如醫師的知覺已被證實會影響醫病互動與照護結果。

§5

Social Cognition & Socio-Emotional Intelligence

社會認知與社會情緒智能

Attribution: our brains automatically explain others' behavior as caused by their disposition (internal) or their situation (external). The medical assistant who charted wrong — careless person, or double shift with a broken workstation? The fundamental attribution error defaults to blaming the person. Safety culture (Week 6) is largely a discipline of resisting that default.

歸因：大腦會自動解釋他人行為的原因——歸給「個人特質」（內在）或「情境」（外在）。記錄出錯的醫療助理，是粗心，還是連上兩班加上當機的工作站？基本歸因謬誤讓我們預設怪罪個人。第六週的安全文化，本質上就是對抗這個預設的紀律。

We also automatically sort people into social categories and over-generalize: stereotype (cognitive: “women aren't strong enough for construction”) → prejudice (emotional attitude) → discrimination (behavior — often illegal). Explicit bias is overt and conscious; implicit bias is hidden even from ourselves, documented in hiring, evaluation, medicine and courts — and we all have bias blind spot: everyone else seems more biased than me.

我們也會自動把人分進社會類別並過度概化：刻板印象（認知：「女性做不了營造」）→ 偏見（情緒態度）→ 歧視（行為——常屬違法）。外顯偏誤公開且自知；內隱偏誤連自己都看不見，已在招募、考核、醫療與司法中被大量記錄——而人人都有偏誤盲點：總覺得別人比我更有偏見。

Emotional empathy 情緒同理

  • Sharing another's feelings — emotional “contagion”感同身受——情緒「感染」

Cognitive empathy 認知同理

  • Knowing and understanding another's perspective理解對方的觀點

Empathic perspective-taking 同理觀點取替

  • Understanding + shared emotion → appropriate action理解＋共感 → 化為適切「行動」
Manager's takeaway
管理者啟示 Giving feedback? Check your attribution first (situation before disposition). Running a layoff? Anticipate social motivations — fairness, status, belonging. Making big decisions? Engineer deliberate thinking: devil's advocates, cooling-off periods, structured criteria. 給回饋前，先檢查歸因（先看情境再怪個人）。處理裁員時，預想公平、地位、歸屬等社會動機。做重大決策時，把深思系統設計進流程：唱反調角色、冷卻期、結構化準則。
