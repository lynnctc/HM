GenAI in Health Care: Uses, Ethics & Law

GenAI 醫療應用與倫理法律

The ChatGPT on your phone is not a medical device — it is a spectacularly fluent text-continuation machine. That single sentence separates safe use from dangerous use of generative AI in hospitals. This week: where GenAI genuinely helps, the six risks every health worker must be able to name, five real-world cautionary cases, Taiwan's regulatory map, and a traffic-light game that trains your judgment.

你手機上的 ChatGPT 不是醫療器材——它是一台極度流暢的「文字接龍機」。這一句話，劃開了生成式 AI 在醫院的安全用法與危險用法。本週內容：GenAI 真正能幫上忙的場域、每位醫療工作者都該叫得出名字的六大風險、五個真實警世案例、台灣的法規地圖，以及一場訓練判斷力的紅綠燈遊戲。

§1

A Text-Continuation Machine Is Not a Medical Device

文字接龍機不是醫療器材

Traditional clinical AI (CADe/CADx) 傳統臨床 AI

  • Classifies/detects: “suspicious lesion here” 分類判讀：「這裡疑似病灶」
  • Task-specific, quantifiable accuracy, clinically validated 任務專一、可量化準確率、經臨床驗證
  • Regulated as a medical device — TFDA registration required 須 TFDA 查驗登記、依醫材管理

Generative AI (LLM / ChatGPT) 生成式 AI

  • Predicts the next most likely token 預測「下一個最可能的字」
  • General, fluent, persuasive — aims to sound right, not be right 通用、流暢、有說服力——目標是「聽起來合理」而非「正確」
  • Phone ChatGPT: no clinical validation, no registration, vendor warns against high-risk use 手機版未經驗證、未查驗登記、開發商明示不用於高風險場域

The regulatory taxonomy of validated tools: CADe (detection — flags and localizes, e.g. lung nodules on CT), CADx (diagnosis — quantitative outputs and risk assessments), CAT (triage — rapid sorting, e.g. brain-bleed alerts in the ER). Taiwan's TFDA guideline for AI/ML CADe & CADx (updated 2025-08) is soft law: the binding rule is that software claiming diagnostic or therapeutic function is a medical device and must be registered. In health care the most dangerous error is not the absurd one — it is the fluent, professional-sounding, wrong one, which is exactly what GenAI produces best.

已驗證工具的法規分類：CADe（偵測——標出病灶位置，如 CT 肺結節）、CADx(診斷——定量數據與風險評估）、CAT（檢傷——快速分流，如急診腦出血警示）。TFDA 的 AI/ML CADe/CADx 指引（2025 年 8 月修正）屬軟法：真正有拘束力的是——宣稱診療功能的軟體即屬醫療器材、須查驗登記。在醫療，最危險的錯不是明顯荒謬的錯，而是「聽起來非常專業、但其實是錯」的錯——這正是 GenAI 最擅長製造的。

§2

Four Places GenAI Shows Up in a Hospital

GenAI 在醫院的四大應用場域

① Admin & documents 行政與文書 🟢

  • Minutes, official letters, patient-education drafts, translation, slides 會議紀錄、公文、衛教初稿、翻譯、簡報
  • Most mature, lowest risk — if no identifiable data goes in and a human signs off 最成熟低風險——前提：不入個資＋人工把關

② Clinical documentation 臨床文件輔助 🟡

  • Ambient scribes drafting notes, discharge summaries, referral letters 環境臨床紀錄、出院摘要、轉診單草擬
  • Iron rule: a draft is only a draft — verify word by word before signing 鐵則：草稿永遠只是草稿，簽名前逐字確認

③ Clinical decision support 臨床決策輔助 🔴

  • Differential brainstorming, drug-interaction queries, literature summaries 鑑別診斷發想、用藥查詢、文獻摘要
  • General GenAI invents drug names, citations, doses — thinking partner, never the answer 通用 GenAI 會杜撰藥名文獻劑量——可當發想夥伴，不可當答案

④ Patient-facing 病人端與衛教 🟡

  • Patients WILL paste their reports into AI — you cannot stop them 病人一定會拿報告去問 AI——你擋不住
  • Opportunity: health literacy, 24/7 FAQs; risk: misinformation, delayed care; new job: guide correct use 機會：識能與全天候解惑；風險：錯誤資訊、延誤就醫；新功課：引導正確使用
§3

Six Risks, Five Real Cases

六大風險、五個真實案例

The six risks every hospital worker should be able to name: ① hallucination — confident fabrication; ② bias — inequity learned and amplified; ③ privacy — data pasted into public AI leaves your control; ④ autonomy — patients' right to know AI took part; ⑤ accountability — “the AI said so” is no defense; ⑥ over-reliance — automation bias eroding judgment. Now the receipts:

每位醫院工作者都該叫得出的六大風險：①幻覺——自信地虛構；②偏差——把不平等學起來並放大；③隱私——貼進公開 AI 的資料離開掌控；④自主——病人有權知道 AI 參與；⑤問責——「AI 說的」不能免責；⑥過度依賴——自動化偏誤侵蝕判斷力。以下是證據：

OpenAI's Whisper, embedded in the Nabla medical transcription product (30,000+ clinicians, ~40 health systems, ~7M visits), was found to fabricate sentences never spoken — including a nonexistent drug, “hyperactivated antibiotics.” Worse: the product auto-deleted source audio for privacy, destroying the audit trail. Michigan researchers found hallucinations in 8 of 10 sampled segments (an ACM FAccT study measured ~1%). Lesson: AI clinical notes are drafts until verified, and convenience must never destroy verifiability.

OpenAI Whisper 被整合進醫療轉錄產品 Nabla（逾 3 萬臨床人員、約 40 個醫療體系、約 700 萬次門診），被發現會憑空生成沒說過的句子——包括不存在的藥「hyperactivated antibiotics」。更糟：產品為隱私自動刪除原始錄音，摧毀了查核路徑。密西根研究者在 10 段抽樣中 8 段發現幻覺（ACM FAccT 研究約 1%）。教訓：AI 臨床紀錄在查核前都是草稿；方便絕不能犧牲可查核性。

A U.S. lawyer filed six precedents supplied by ChatGPT — all fabricated; when asked, ChatGPT insisted they were real and findable on Westlaw. US$5,000 sanction (S.D.N.Y. 2023). Swap the domain: the same mechanism invents clinical guidelines, doses, and study conclusions in perfect formatting. Every fact, citation and dose must be traced to its source — AI's strength is ideation, not verification.

美國律師引用 ChatGPT 提供的六件判例——全屬虛構；律師反問時它仍堅稱屬實、可在 Westlaw 查到。法院裁罰五千美元（紐約南區，2023）。換到醫療：同一機制會以完美格式杜撰臨床指引、劑量與研究結論。每個事實、文獻、劑量都必須回查原始來源——AI 的長處是發想，不是查證。

A U.S. risk algorithm affecting tens of millions used past medical spending as a proxy for health need (Science, 2019). Disadvantaged groups use less care → spend less → get scored “healthier” → miss out on extra care. Fixing the bias raised the share of Black patients receiving additional care from 17.7% to 46.5%. Ask of every tool: was it validated on my patient population — and what proxies hide inside it? What biases might a model trained on Taiwan's NHI data inherit?

一套影響數千萬人的美國風險演算法用「過去醫療花費」代理健康需求（Science, 2019）。弱勢族群就醫少→花費低→被判定「較健康」→更難獲得加強照護。修正後，獲加強照護的黑人病患比例由 17.7% 升至 46.5%。對每個工具都要問：它在「我的」病人族群驗證過嗎？裡面藏著什麼代理變數？用台灣健保資料訓練的模型，可能藏著我們自己的哪些偏差？

Within 20 days of allowing ChatGPT in 2023, Samsung logged three confidential leaks (source code, test code, meeting transcripts) and banned GenAI company-wide. Hospital version: pasting a named patient's course into public AI to draft a discharge summary. Medical records are “special category” personal data under Taiwan's PDPA — maximum protection. De-identification is the floor, not a suggestion; identifiable data only in closed, approved environments. And is masking the name alone truly de-identification?

2023 年三星開放 ChatGPT 後 20 天內發生三起機密外洩（原始碼、測試程式、會議紀錄），隨即全面禁用。醫院版本：為趕出院摘要，把含姓名病歷號的病程貼進公開 AI。病歷屬個資法「特種個人資料」，保護最嚴。去識別化是底線而非建議；可識別資料只能在院內封閉、核可的環境處理。只遮姓名，算真正去識別化嗎？

Balance matters: objective-EHR studies show AI documentation tools cut note-writing time ~20.4% per visit (10.3→8.2 min); an RCT found ~2.5 fewer after-hours documentation hours weekly and lower burnout; NEJM AI (Atrium/Wake Forest) reported 24% faster drafting and +11.3 patients/day — though system-level metrics didn't uniformly improve. With human verification, auditability, and informed consent in place, the benefits are real. Your call: which three conditions would you set before approving deployment?

平衡也重要：使用客觀病歷資料的研究顯示 AI 文件工具使每診紀錄時間降約 20.4%（10.3→8.2 分）；RCT 顯示每週減約 2.5 小時下班後文書並降低倦怠；NEJM AI（Atrium／Wake Forest）報告草擬快 24%、每日多看 11.3 位病人——但組織層級指標未一致改善。在「人工查核、可回溯、知情同意」三者到位時，效益是真實的。換你決定：核准導入前，你會設哪三個前提？

The AI Basic Act's seven principles: sustainability & well-being, human autonomy, privacy & data governance, security & safety, transparency & explainability, fairness & non-discrimination, accountability. Its clock: within 6 months government AI-use risk assessments; 12 months usage rules and internal controls; 24 months every ministry reviews its statutes. Milestone already passed: Taiwan's NHI began covering its first AI medical device (anesthesia risk monitoring) at the end of 2023. The two red lines to memorize: PDPA — identifiable patient data into public AI is high-risk illegal processing; Medical Care/Physicians Acts — the tool doesn't go to court, the person using it does.

AI 基本法七大原則：永續發展與福祉、人類自主、隱私保護與資料治理、資安與安全、透明與可解釋、公平與不歧視、問責。施行時鐘：6 個月內政府完成既有 AI 使用風險評估；12 個月內訂使用規範與內控；24 個月內各部會檢討修法。已發生的里程碑：健保於 2023 年底首度給付 AI 醫材（麻醉高風險手術病人監測）。要背下來的兩條紅線：個資法——可識別病人資料貼進公開 AI 是高風險違法個資處理；醫療法／醫師法——工具不會上法庭，用工具的人會。

§5

The Traffic-Light Game

紅綠燈遊戲

Hospital GenAI rules in three colors — 🔴 forbidden, 🟡 allowed with verification, 🟢 encouraged. Sort each staff behavior. Then, before any real use, run the five questions: Is anything identifiable in my input? Will the output touch a patient directly? Is the tool hospital-approved? What happens if this is wrong? Should the patient be told AI took part?

院內 GenAI 三色規則——🔴 禁止、🟡 須查核、🟢 鼓勵。分類下列行為。實際使用前，先跑五個問題：輸入有可識別資訊嗎？輸出會直接用在病人身上嗎？工具是院方核可的嗎？錯了會有什麼後果？病人需要知道 AI 參與了嗎？

Manager's three moves
管理者三行動 ① Inventory what AI tools staff actually use (more than you think). ② Write policy — approved-tool lists beat blanket bans, which only push use underground. ③ Train — AI literacy in continuing education: judgment and red lines, not prompt tricks. The future isn't AI replacing clinicians; it's clinicians who can use AI, assisting those who can't. The stronger AI gets, the more your judgment is worth. ①盤點院內各單位實際在用的 AI 工具（比你想的多）。②訂政策——核可清單勝過一律禁止（禁止只會逼到檯面下）。③做培訓——把 AI 素養納入在職教育：教判斷與紅線，不是教 prompt 技巧。未來不是 AI 取代醫護，而是會用 AI 的醫護協助不太會用的。AI 越強，你的「判斷」越值錢。
