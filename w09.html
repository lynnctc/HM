Financing Health Care & the NHI

Every management decision eventually meets a payment system — and payment systems change behavior more reliably than any memo. §1

How Insurance Works — and Its Two Demons

保險怎麼運作——與它的兩隻惡魔

Insurance transfers risk to an insurer in exchange for a premium. The insurer runs a risk assessment (underwriting) to estimate probability and dollar value of losses, adds a loading cost (overhead + profit), and prices the contract. Risk can be spread by community rating (everyone pays the same premium regardless of health) or group rating (premium reflects an employer group's risk — younger workforce, cheaper premium).

保險是把風險移轉給保險人、換取保費。保險人執行風險評估（核保）估計損失的機率與金額，加上附加費用（營運＋利潤）後訂價。分散風險有兩種費率：社區費率（不論健康狀況、人人同價）與團體費率（依雇主團體風險訂價——員工越年輕保費越低）。

Moral hazard 道德風險

  • Once costs are borne by someone else, people take more risks / use more care成本由他人承擔後，人會更冒險、用更多醫療
  • Occurs after insurance is bought 發生在投保「之後」

Adverse selection 逆選擇

  • The sickest are the most eager to buy insurance — the pool fills with high-risk members and can bankrupt the insurer最可能用到保險的人最想投保——保險池充滿高風險者，可能拖垮保險公司
  • Occurs before/at enrollment 發生在投保「當下之前」

Cost-Sharing Calculator

部分負擔計算機

Insurers push part of each bill back to the patient — precisely to fight moral hazard. Three levers: copayment (fixed amount per visit), deductible (you pay everything until a yearly threshold), and coinsurance (you pay a percentage of what remains). Move the numbers and watch who pays what.

保險人把帳單的一部分推回給病人——正是為了對抗道德風險。三個槓桿：定額負擔（每次就醫固定金額）、自負額（年度門檻內全自付）、定率負擔（其餘金額按比例分攤）。調整數字，看誰付多少。

Taiwan note
台灣對照 Taiwan's NHI relies mainly on fixed copayments (定額) with caps and exemptions — not deductibles. The 2023 copay reform raised drug and ER copays at large hospitals partly to redirect mild cases to clinics: cost-sharing as a referral policy tool. 台灣健保主要採「定額」部分負擔（含上限與免除），沒有自負額制。112 年部分負擔新制調高大醫院藥品與急診負擔，用意之一是把輕症導回基層：部分負擔也是分級醫療的政策工具。
§3

How You Pay Doctors Is What You Get

怎麼付錢給醫師，就得到什麼醫療

Because of information asymmetry, physicians can shift patient demand — supplier-induced demand (SID) — and third-party payment removes the patient's price restraint. So the payment method becomes the steering wheel. RBRVS (adopted by U.S. Medicare in 1992, and used in Taiwan's fee schedule) prices each service by the resources it takes: physician work + practice expense + liability insurance. 由於資訊不對稱，醫師能左右病人的需求——供給者誘發需求（SID）——而第三方支付又拿掉了病人的價格自制。於是「支付方式」成了方向盤。RBRVS（美國 Medicare 1992 年採用，台灣支付標準亦引進）按資源成本為每項服務訂價：醫師工作＋執業成本＋醫責保險。點選各支付制度，看它創造的誘因：

§4

Taiwan Spotlight: the NHI, Warts and All

台灣視角：全民健保的光與影

Since 1995, Taiwan's NHI has been a compulsory, single-payer social insurance: risk-sharing rather than risk-rated premiums, payroll-based contributions (current rate 5.17% plus a 2.11% supplementary premium — the deck's 4.91% predates the 2021 adjustment), redistributive by design, covering basic care for over 99% of the population. Financing principles: adequacy, fairness (horizontal and vertical), neutrality, stability.

1995 年開辦的全民健保是強制性、單一保險人的社會保險：共同分擔風險而非依風險訂價，以薪資為基礎收費（現行費率 5.17%＋補充保費 2.11%——投影片的 4.91% 是 2021 年調整前的數字），制度性所得重分配，為逾 99% 人口提供基本照護。財源四原則：充足、公平（水平與垂直）、中立、穩定。

Wins: coverage 58%→99%+, catastrophic-illness patients' financial burden slashed, better resource distribution, mountain/island access programs. Warts: payroll-base design debates (base salary vs full salary), occupational-union members underpaying relative to region-insured groups.

亮點：納保率 58%→99%+、重大傷病經濟負擔大減、資源分布更公平、山地離島醫療計畫。陰影：投保薪資設計爭議（本俸 vs 全薪）、職業工會保費遠低於地區人口。

Annual medical-cost growth tamed from 15% to ~7.5%; admissions and lengths of stay controlled. But FFS still fuels waste, outpatient utilization keeps climbing, and fee-schedule/drug-price distortions persist.

醫療費用年成長率由 15% 降至約 7.5%；住院率與住院日受控。但論量計酬仍助長浪費、門診利用率持續上升、支付標準與藥價基準未盡合理。

Satisfaction with outpatient and inpatient care is high and hospitals push QI programs — but uneven fee schedules distort specialty choices, review mechanisms are contested, and physician workloads stay heavy enough to cap quality gains.

門住診滿意度高、醫院普遍推 QI——但科別支付不均扭曲選科、審查制度屢有爭議、醫師服務量過大使品質難再上層樓。

Pros: maximal risk pooling (law of large numbers), no adverse selection against the weak, monopsony bargaining power, low administrative costs, equal treatment across statuses, easier audit and regional planning. Cons: inflexibility of benefits and finances, hard to satisfy special needs, weak efficiency incentives in a public monopoly, and politically exposed decision making — premium hikes and payment penalties bend to politics.

優點：風險分擔效率最高（大數法則）、弱勢不被逆選擇排除、獨買議價力、行政成本低、身分別待遇一致、稽核與區域規劃容易。缺點：給付與財務缺乏彈性、難滿足特殊需求、公營獨占缺乏效率動力、決策易受政治干預——費率調整與懲處常向政治低頭。

Structural deficit as the population ages and new drugs/technology arrive; revenue and expenditure decided by separate mechanisms (weak linkage); opaque quality information for the public; drug-price squeezes discouraging new/original drugs; and the rationing question no one can dodge: should a life-saving drug costing millions enter the benefit package — at whose expense?

人口老化與新藥新科技帶來結構性收支失衡；收入與支出由不同機制決定、缺乏連動；醫療品質資訊不透明；藥價壓低造成新藥原廠藥卻步；以及誰都躲不掉的配給難題：天價救命藥該不該納入給付——排擠誰？
